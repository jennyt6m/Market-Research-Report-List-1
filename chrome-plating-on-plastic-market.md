<p><p>The chrome plating on plastic market encompasses two main types: traditional plating and electroless plating. Traditional plating involves electrolytic processes where a metallic layer is deposited on the plastic substrate, providing a shiny, durable finish. In contrast, electroless plating relies on chemical reactions to deposit metal uniformly without an electric current, offering advantages in complex shapes and surfaces. Both methods enhance the aesthetic appeal and functionality of plastic products, making them ideal for automotive, electronics, and consumer goods applications.</p></p>
